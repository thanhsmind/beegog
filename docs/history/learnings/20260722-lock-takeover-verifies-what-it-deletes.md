# 2026-07-22 — A rename wins a path, not a file: the stale-takeover bug that broke mutual exclusion

**Feature:** release-1-8-0 (cell rel180-4) · **Tags:** [locks, concurrency, ci, windows, release]

1. **The bug.** `tryStaleTakeover` judged a lock stale from a snapshot, then `renameSync`'d the lock away and deleted whatever it had renamed. `rename` guarantees only that one racer consumes a given *path*; it says nothing about the *content* at that path still being the file you judged. A slow racer's stale verdict could outlive reality: a faster racer completed its takeover, wrote a fresh live lock, entered its critical section — and the slow racer then renamed away and deleted that legitimate lock. Both believed they held it. **Rule:** any takeover/reclaim that acts on an earlier judgement must re-verify identity (pid + token + timestamp) of the thing it actually consumed, and undo on mismatch. Judge and claim are two steps; treating them as one is the whole bug.
2. **The same shape had already bitten twice that week** — `sweepExpiredClaims` reclaiming a live heartbeating owner (rel180-2), and the archive-vs-append lost write (dp-3). All three are read-then-act on shared state. **Rule:** when a decision and its mutation are separated by any I/O, ask what a competitor could have changed in between, and either hold one lock across both or verify identity at the mutation.
3. **Local green is not the gate; exact-tag CI on every platform is.** The full chain was green three consecutive local runs, and the tag's CI was still red on both platforms — one for the mutual-exclusion failure (2-core contention), one for a Windows `EPERM` on the new decisions lock. The v1.8.0 tag was re-pointed to the fix commit, the established pattern.
4. **Windows portability is a per-module property, not a repo-wide one.** `claims.mjs` got a bounded transient-fs retry (EBUSY/EPERM/ENOTEMPTY) in rel1710rc-5; `lock.mjs` never did, so the first new consumer of the lock layer (dp-3's decisions lock) re-discovered it on Windows. **Rule:** when a platform fix lands in one module, sweep the sibling modules that perform the same fs mutations.
5. **Both bugs were pinned deterministically, not chased with load.** Forced microtask interleaving for the takeover race; a stubbed throwing fs call for the Windows EPERM — proving a Windows-only failure on Linux. **Rule:** a race fix that cannot be made to fail on demand has not been understood yet.
